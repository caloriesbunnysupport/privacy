6. USE OF FOOD PHOTOGRAPHS AND AI PROCESSING
When you submit a food photograph or text description through the Application for nutritional analysis, the data is transmitted to the Google Gemini API for AI-powered processing. We wish to be fully transparent about the following:
Food photographs submitted for analysis are transmitted to Google's servers for processing by the Gemini AI model. We do not store original food photographs on our own servers beyond the period necessary to transmit them to the Gemini API, unless you explicitly save an entry to your dietary log.
The nutritional analysis results returned by the Gemini API are stored in your dietary log on our Supabase database infrastructure if you choose to save them.
Google's processing of data submitted through the Gemini API is governed by Google's API Terms of Service and applicable data processing agreements between us and Google.
We do not use your food photographs to train AI models without your explicit consent. We do not share your food photographs with third parties other than Google (via the Gemini API) in the course of providing the nutritional analysis service.

COOKIE AND TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES POLICY
CaloriesBunny (モグダイエット)
Effective Date: April 12, 2025
Last Updated: April 12, 2025
This Cookie and Tracking Technologies Policy ("Cookie Policy") explains how CaloriesBunny / モグダイエット (the "Application") uses cookies, session tokens, device identifiers, server-side logging, and similar tracking technologies. This Cookie Policy forms part of and is incorporated into our Privacy Policy and Terms and Conditions.
While CaloriesBunny is a mobile application and does not use traditional browser cookies in the same way as websites, the Application and its associated backend infrastructure employ equivalent tracking and identification mechanisms. This Policy is intended to provide full transparency about those mechanisms in compliance with the Act on the Protection of Personal Information (個人情報の保護に関する法律, "APPI"), the guidelines of the Personal Information Protection Commission ("PPC"), and applicable best practices for mobile application data transparency.
1. WHAT ARE TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES?
Tracking technologies are small files, identifiers, or technical mechanisms used to identify a device, maintain session state, collect usage data, or monitor application performance. In the context of a mobile application like CaloriesBunny, these include:
Session Tokens: Cryptographic tokens generated upon user login to authenticate subsequent API requests. These are stored temporarily on your device and expire upon logout or after a defined inactivity period.
Device Identifiers: Unique identifiers associated with your mobile device, which may be collected by our error monitoring service (Sentry) to associate crash reports and diagnostic events with a specific device.
Server Logs: Our backend infrastructure (via Supabase) and error monitoring service (Sentry) automatically log certain technical metadata, including IP addresses and request timestamps, when you interact with the Application.
Caching Identifiers: Our session caching layer (Upstash) may store temporary session keys to maintain application state between API calls and improve response times.
2. TYPES OF TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES USED
2.1 Strictly Necessary / Functional Technologies:
These technologies are essential for the operation of the Application and cannot be disabled without significantly impairing functionality.
Authentication Tokens (Supabase): JSON Web Tokens (JWTs) or equivalent session credentials issued by Supabase upon login. These are required to authenticate your identity when accessing protected features and data within the Application. Legal basis: contractual necessity.
Session Cache Keys (Upstash): Short-lived Redis keys used to cache API responses and session state, improving Application performance and reducing latency. These expire automatically after a short period of inactivity. Legal basis: contractual necessity.
API Request Identifiers: Unique identifiers included in API requests to Supabase and other backend services to route requests to the correct user record and ensure data integrity. Legal basis: contractual necessity.
2.2 Performance and Diagnostic Technologies:
These technologies collect information about how the Application performs on your device in order to identify and resolve technical issues.
Sentry Error Tracking: Sentry is an error monitoring service provided by Functional Software, Inc. When the Application encounters an error, crash, or unexpected condition, Sentry automatically captures a diagnostic report that may include: the type and nature of the error, the Application version, the operating system version, coarse device model information, device identifiers (as configured in our Sentry implementation), IP address, and technical stack trace data. This data is transmitted to Sentry's servers and is used exclusively to identify, diagnose, and resolve software defects. Sentry data is not used for marketing or advertising purposes. Legal basis: legitimate interests (maintaining Application stability and quality).
2.3 Server-Side Logging:
Our backend infrastructure hosted on Supabase and associated cloud services automatically generates server-side logs when you make API requests. These logs may include:
Timestamp of request
IP address of the requesting device
Type of API request
HTTP response status code
Approximate data volume of request and response
Server logs are retained for up to 30 days and are used solely for security monitoring, abuse prevention, and service reliability purposes. They are not used to build marketing profiles or track individual users across services. Legal basis: legitimate interests.
2.4 AI Processing Identifiers:
When food photographs or text descriptions are submitted to the Google Gemini API for nutritional analysis, technical request identifiers are generated for the purposes of tracking the request status and ensuring correct attribution of AI-generated responses to the requesting user session. These identifiers are transient and are not retained beyond the processing lifecycle of the individual API call, except where the nutritional analysis result is saved to your dietary log by your deliberate action. Legal basis: contractual necessity.
3. THIRD-PARTY TRACKING BY SERVICE PROVIDERS
Our third-party service providers may independently implement tracking technologies as part of their services. We summarize these below:
Sentry (Functional Software, Inc.): May collect device identifiers and IP addresses as part of crash and error reporting. Governed by Sentry's Privacy Policy, available at https://sentry.io/privacy/.
Supabase (Supabase, Inc.): Server-side logging of API requests including IP addresses. Governed by Supabase's Privacy Policy, available at https://supabase.com/privacy.
Google LLC (Gemini API): API request data transmitted for AI analysis may be subject to Google's data processing practices. Governed by Google's Privacy Policy and API Terms of Service.
Upstash: Session and cache data handling. Governed by Upstash's Privacy Policy, available at https://upstash.com/trust/privacy.pdf.
Apple Inc. / Google LLC (App Store Platforms): In-app purchase processing and platform-level analytics. Governed by Apple's and Google's respective privacy policies.
We do not control the tracking technologies deployed by third-party service providers and encourage you to review their respective privacy and cookie policies for full information.
4. WHAT WE DO NOT DO
For the avoidance of doubt, we wish to be explicit that:
We do not use advertising cookies, advertising SDKs, or behavioral advertising tracking technologies within the Application.
We do not share tracking data or device identifiers with advertising networks or data brokers.
We do not use cross-application tracking technologies to monitor your activity outside of the CaloriesBunny Application.
We do not use fingerprinting techniques to identify users who have not logged into an account.
We do not deploy third-party social media tracking pixels within the Application.
5. YOUR CHOICES AND CONTROLS
Because the tracking technologies used in CaloriesBunny are largely essential to the operation of the Application, there is limited scope to disable them without impairing functionality. However, the following controls are available to you:
Logout: Logging out of the Application invalidates your authentication session token, preventing further authenticated API requests until you log in again.
Account Deletion: Deleting your account through the Application settings removes your personal information from our systems (subject to legal retention obligations) and terminates all active session tokens associated with your account.
Sentry Opt-Out: If you wish to opt out of diagnostic crash reporting through Sentry, please contact us at caloriesbunnysupport@gmail.com. We will use reasonable efforts to disable Sentry data capture for your account where technically feasible.
Device Settings: You may be able to reset your advertising identifier through your device's operating system settings (iOS: Settings > Privacy > Tracking; Android: Settings > Privacy > Ads), which may limit the device identifiers available to our service providers.
6. DATA RETENTION FOR TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES
The following retention periods apply to data collected through tracking technologies:
Authentication session tokens: Expire upon logout or after 1hour of inactivity, whichever is earlier.
Upstash cache keys: Automatically expire after a short period (typically minutes to hours) of inactivity.
Sentry diagnostic reports: Retained for up to 90 days in accordance with Sentry's standard retention configuration.
Server access logs (Supabase): Retained for up to 30 days.
Tracking data is not retained beyond these periods unless required by applicable law.
